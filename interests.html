My Interests and Goals

Outside of classes and technical projects, two things I’m passionate about are investing and fitness. I spend a lot of my free time researching the stock market, following financial news, and testing different strategies for long-term growth. Over the past year, I’ve been studying how different sectors respond to broader economic trends and how to manage risk through diversification. It’s been both challenging and rewarding — I’ve learned a lot about balancing short-term fluctuations with long-term conviction, and I’ve managed to grow my portfolio by roughly 30% year-over-year so far. My next goal is to build a more structured investment plan, focus on learning fundamental analysis in depth, and track performance more consistently through data visualization tools.

When I’m not looking at charts or reading market reports, I love going to the gym. Working out is my way to reset — it keeps me focused, disciplined, and motivated. I usually train five days a week, mixing weight training with cardio. Fitness has taught me the same principles that apply to investing: patience, consistency, and commitment. Whether I’m lifting weights or analyzing stock trends, I enjoy the process of setting measurable goals and tracking progress over time. Both areas challenge me to improve a little bit every day, and I find that incredibly fulfilling.
